Sea Turtle Species

Green Turtle


Known for their herbivorous diet and vibrant green fat tissue, green turtles are found in tropical and subtropical waters worldwide. They undertake long migrations and are important for seagrass and coral reef ecosystems

Leatherback Turtle


The largest of all sea turtles, leatherbacks lack a hard shell and have a unique leathery skin. They have a global distribution and undertake the longest migrations of any marine reptile, diving to great depths in search of their jellyfish prey.

Loggerhead Turtle


With large heads and powerful jaws, loggerheads have a wide distribution in both temperate and tropical regions. They are known for their strong nesting instincts and play a crucial role in maintaining balanced marine food webs.

Hawksbill Turtle


Hawksbill turtles have a distinctive beak-like mouth and colorful shell. They inhabit coral reefs and coastal areas, and their diet primarily consists of sponges. They are critically endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Olive Ridley Turtle


Olive ridleys have olive-colored shells and inhabit warm waters, particularly in the Pacific and Indian Oceans. They are known for their mass nesting events, known as arribadas, where thousands of females come ashore to lay eggs simultaneously.

Kemp's Ridley Turtle


Kemp's ridleys are the smallest and most endangered sea turtle species. They primarily inhabit the Gulf of Mexico and are known for their synchronized nesting behavior. Conservation efforts focus on protecting their nesting beaches and reducing incidental capture in fishing gear.

Flatback Turtle


Found exclusively in the waters around Australia, flatbacks have a unique flattened shell. They primarily nest on sandy beaches in northern Australia and their diet consists of soft-bodied invertebrates.
