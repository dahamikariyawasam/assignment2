BENTOTA

SEA TURTLE HATCHERY AND RESCUE CENTRE.

Bentota Turtle Egg Care Center

Bentota Turtle Hatchery was established by Sri Lankans with the aim of saving turtles from extinction.
Previously, people usually collected turtle eggs by fishermen and sold them to higher sellers.
With the establishment of the turtle hatchery, these eggs were fed and cared for for 2 to 4 years.

What we do!


We collect the turtle eggs and provide them with proper care and release them back to their usual habitat after hatching.

Collecting Eggs

Our center works with trained individuals or organizations dedicated to turtle conservation to collect sea turtle eggs, which includes monitoring nesting beaches and identifying turtle nests during the nesting period.

Caring for Eggs

Caring for sea turtle eggs is a critical component of turtle conservation efforts. Once the eggs are collected from the nests, they are handled very carefully to avoid any damage or rotation of the eggs. We provide proper temperature, humidity and ventilation to create an environment that mimics the natural conditions required for eggs.

Releasing the cubs to their usual habitats.

After the hatching of the turtles, except for the weak ones, the rest of the hatchlings are released to their usual habitats. We also provide the care needed for the weak baby turtles.


By joining us, you can gain valuable experiences.

Visiting the Bentota Turtle Egg Care Center will give you the opportunity to spend time with baby turtles. Here you will also be able to learn about the eggs of baby turtles and their young life.
